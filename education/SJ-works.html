SJ Works

SJ Works offers students from low-income and underserved communities their first jobs to set them on a path to success.
SJ Works creates meaningful youth employment opportunities to help at-risk and underserved youth build towards college and careers. Despite San José’s rapidly expanding economy, youth unemployment rates still exceed 20% in many San José neighborhoods, correlating with the national trend. Young people from low-income families are hit the hardest with a 20-point differential in employment rates between low-income families and their wealthier peers.
A job for any young person can be the first step in the pathway to long-term success. By investing in pathways to employment for at-risk youth between the ages of 14-29, particularly those in gang-impacted neighborhoods, the city intends to broaden opportunities for many teens who struggle against the Valley's widening economic gap, and make San José safer. Teen training and job placement programs have the power to reach disconnected youth and create pathways to positive outcomes. Simultaneously, many employers indicate a desire to hire locally and train youth and young adults, but don’t have the staff to guide youth and young adults in financial literacy and other life skills. Investing in job-readiness and employment experiences can lead to the additional schooling and skill-building needed for career-track employment. Working in partnership with employers, schools and community-based organizations, SJ Works piloted new strategies to develop a scalable and sustainable system to deliver youth employment opportunities, particularly during the summer.

To apply for a summer job or internship go to the work2future Foundation page on SJ Works.
