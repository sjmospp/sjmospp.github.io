School/City Collaborative

Established in 1997, the School/City Collaborative is comprised of school district superintendents, the Mayor, Council members and City administration. The mission of the S/CC is to develop sustaining partnerships to maximize resources to serve our children, families and the community and the educational, development, and economic growth of our community.

The School/City Collaborative operates under the following Guiding Principles:

  1. Focus on the long term beneifits of families and Students in San José
  2. Operate in a cooperative manner which leverages the strengths of both the City and the school districts
  3. Work in a proactive manner with open and honest dialogue
  4. Focus on 3-5 mutually identified priorities, with established measurable outcomes
  5. Be understanding of each systems challenge and constraints
  6. Be committed to result and be innovative in its solutions and
  7. Utilize our collective strengths to have a strong advocacy voice

City staff and school district superintendents have identified the following specific areas of focus for the 2017-18 S/CC work plan:

  1. Implement a Joint Use Facilities strategy and leverage the Greenprint plan to designate geographic "hotspots" where residents are most in need of open space.
  2. Digital Infrastructure: Increase WiFi and broadband access for students and their families in areas of greatest need.
  3. Digital Literacy: Identify collaborative ways to increase educational programming for both students and families around safely and effectively using the Internet for learning.
  4. Preschool: Increase the availability of quality preschool opportunities for families in San José, with a goal of ensuring that every child enters kindergarten ready to learn.
  5. Out-of-School Learning (Afterschool and Summer): Expand learning opportunities for students beyond the school day by focusing on providing quality programs during afterschool and summer hours. Programs that extend and build upon in-school learning will be identified, assessed, and proposed for expansion.
  6. College and Career Readiness: Develop targeted programs that increase the rates of graduation, college admission, and workforce readiness for San José students. Where successful programs exist, build capacity to increase the number of students served.
  7. Status Reports: Truancy and School Policing. Monitor truancy and school policing issues and coordinate with school districts, school site staff, and related law enforcement agencies on a response.
