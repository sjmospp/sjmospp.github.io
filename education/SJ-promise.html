SJ Promise

Partnering with Evergreen Community College and West Valley College, SJ Promise provides up to two years of education to over 1,500 low-income and underrepresented San José high school graduates.

On March 8, 2017, Mayor Liccardo launched San José Promise in partnership with San José Evergreen Community College District and West Valley College. San José Promise will allow us to take strides toward making college an attainable goal and realizable expectation for every San José high school graduate. Over the next two academic years, over 1,500 low-income and underrepresented San José high school graduates will have access to up to two years of free community college, along with the academic and social supports to successfully complete a two-year degree, a career technical certificate, or transfer to a four-year university.

Today, only 30% of students who enter San José high schools will earn a career technical certificate, an Associate’s Degree, or a Bachelor’s Degree. Of the 30% of students who have success, it takes an average of 6 years to do so. The vision for the San José Promise is to increase the college success rate by 50%, where reducing the time it takes for students to complete a degree is critical factor.

With SJ Promise, San José joins leading cities around the state and country that have also launched Promise programs.
