Wellbeing Project

Measuring wellbeing allows the City of San José is able to capture the conditions of the social, physical, and economic environments and gain a qualitative understanding of the cumulative impact of all that our city does.

What is Wellbeing?

Wellbeing is commonly assumed to be happiness or wellness, however, wellbeing goes beyond those aspects of satisfaction. Wellbeing is having the skills, characteristics, and capacities to flourish as an individual and thrive as a community. It is a multidimensional measurement of a community’s strengths and needs. Local government has a critical role to play in causes and conditions that support or detract from wellbeing.

Why measure Wellbeing?

San José recognizes that economic growth alone does not determine a community’s success. True measures of progress take into account the wellbeing of people. Do they feel empowered to make change? Are they happy, healthy, and connected to the community? Are they able to access lifelong learning? Clean air? Without these measures, governments are challenged to manage for better results. By measuring wellbeing, the City of San José is able to move beyond the traditional measures capturing the conditions of the social, physical, and economic environments, and gaining a qualitative understanding of the cumulative impact of all that our city does. Wellbeing is a dynamic way of determining more quickly and precisely what will make San José thrive.
