Education

We are focusing Silicon Valley’s ingenuity to level the educational access playing field with a cradle-to-career education and workforce strategy. Mayor Liccardo has launched initiatives designed to:
  • Create partnerships with families, communities, and school districts
  • Improve alignment between city services, nonprofit education organizations, and schools
  • Develop activities and benchmarks to measure tangible impacts and improvements to our children's education
These strategies will leverage the City as a Classroom for lifelong learning.

SJ Promise

Partnering with Evergreen Community College and West Valley College, SJ Promise provides up to two years of education to over 1,500 low-income and underrepresented San José high school graduates.

SJ Learns

SJ Learns is an after-school learning program that provides high-quality enrichment opportunities and offers K-3 students a supportive, safe place to spend their afternoons. In its first year, the program served 650+ students from 16 schools in low-income and underserved neighborhoods.

LEARN MORE

SJ Works

SJ Works offers students from low-income and underserved communities their first jobs to set them on a path to success.

My Brother's Keeper

The City of San José gladly joined President Obama’s nationwide initiative that seeks to help young men of color receive opportunities to succeed–regardless of circumstances.

School/City Collaborative

A collaboration between the Mayor, Council members and City administration, S/CC develops partnerships to maximize resources to serve our children, families and the community and the educational, development, and economic growth of our community

Coding 5K Challenge

The Coding 5K Challenge will increase participation in coding camps and classes at Public Libraries, Community Centers, and schools to 5,000 youth a year, particularly in underrepresented neighborhoods.
