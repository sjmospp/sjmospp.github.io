About

The Mayor’s Strategic Initiatives and Budget team brings expertise from the Public, Corporate and Noprofit sectors. The team's strategic approach to the work is to partner, to be evidence-based, innovative, transparent and simple. We look forward to working with you.

Khanh Duy Russo

Director of Strategic Partnerships

Khanh Duy Russo is the Director of Strategic Partnerships and the Mayor's Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, Transgender Liaison to the Community. In that role, he develops strategic initiatives with partners that leverages, community resources and investments for creative, innovative public problem solving. Prior to joining the Mayor's office, he worked at Cisco where he guided the global strategy and implementation of internet and technology solutions addressing critical human needs. This included strategies to alleviate hunger and homelessness, increase access to water and disaster relief. He holds a Master’s degree in Public Policy and Management from Carnegie Mellon University, a Bachelor’s degree in Political Science and Religious Studies from Santa Clara University, and was a Coro Northern California Public Affairs Fellow. Khanh speaks fluent Vietnamese and is conversant in Spanish. He and his husband currently reside in Downtown San José.

Nicholas Almeida

Budget Director and Chief Service Officer

Nicholas Almeida is the Budget Director and Chief Service Officer. In this dual role, he facilitates the development and implementation the Mayor’s annual budget priorities and develops a vision to reimagine the City as a City of Service leader - setting a new standard for community and corporate involvement in tackling the Citys toughest problems. Prior to this role, Nicholas served as an Executive Fellow for the City where he assisted in the development of a workforce and entrepreneurship center addressing the unique needs of the City’s diverse populations. Nicholas is also the co-founder of an impact investing firm; served as a senior leader at a foundation where he led new strategic initiatives and corporate social responsibility efforts; and teaches social entrepreneurship as an adjunct professor. Nicholas other experience includes leadership roles in public finance, program evaluation, and government auditing. Nicholas graduated from Stanford University with a Bachelor’s degree in Public Policy and a Master’s degree in Sociology.

Ingrid Holguin

Chief Learning Officer

Ingrid Holguin joined the Office of the Mayor of San José, Sam Liccardo as Policy Advisor for Strategic Partnerships in early 2015. Ms. Holguin has over 20 years of experience working on issues of economic and community development with a passion for building financial capabilities for individuals and institutions. Most recently she was Director of Savings Programs for Opportunity Fund, a San José-headquartered Community Development Financial Institution. As a consultant for over ten years and prior work as Program Officer for the Ford Foundation in Mexico City, working in Mexico and Central America, her work has included philanthropy, impact investing, strategic planning, evaluation and program design. Ms. Holguin holds an MBA from the University of California – Berkeley, Walter A. Haas School of Business and a B.A. in Economics and Management from Ball State University. She speaks fluent Spanish, lives in San José with her husband and teenage daughter and is proud mother of an AmeriCorps VISTA Teaching Fellow at an East San José middle school extended learning program.

Anna Le

Fund Development and Strategic Planning Manager

Anna Le serves as a Fund Development and Strategic Planning Manager for the Vietnamese American Cultural Center..

She brings over 20 years of experience in project management, community outreach, economic and business development, fund development, strategic planning, organizational leadership, community engagement, administration, human resources, budgeting, purchasing, grant writing, and event management. Her diverse experience includes demonstrated excellence in establishing and maintaining working relationships with private and public sectors, nonprofits, elected officials, coalitions, consultants, developers, real estate and financing professionals, contractors, volunteers and other stakeholders and organizations. Her experience includes being Administrative /Human Resource Manager with Everett Charles Technology, Associate Developer with Eden Housing, Development Officer with San José Redevelopment Agency, and Program Manager with San José Department of Transportation.

She has a Master’s degree in Urban and Regional Planning and a Bachelor’s degree in Mathematics from San José State University. She speaks fluent Vietnamese and resides in San José with her husband and two children.

Dima Khoury

Gen2Gen Campaign Director

Dima Khoury is the Gen2Gen Campaign Director. An accomplished business operations leader with extensive background in technology shei has over 20 years of experience in high tech companies, leading software engineering teams as Director of engineering and as a Director of business operations. She retired from Cisco October 2014 and is now focused on leveraging her knowledge and experience for the nonprofit sector. Candace Le

Policy Advisor for Education and Workforce Initiatives

Candace Le is a Policy Advisor in Education and Workforce Initiatives. A native Vietnamese speaker, Candace is deeply rooted in and connected to the Vietnamese-American community locally. She received a Bachelor's degree from University of California - Berkeley in Sociology with an emphasis on the impact of education in minority communities. Prior to joining Mayor Liccardo’s team, Candace was the program coordinator for an afterschool youth development program at Asian Americans for Community Involvement. Candace continues to be involved as an organizer with community-based organizations and works to connect her direct-service understanding and local government experience to better serve the diverse San José community.
