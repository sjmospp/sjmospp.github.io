About

The Mayor’s Strategic Partnerships and Performance team brings expertise from the Public, Corporate and Noprofit sectors. The team's strategic approach to the work is to partner, to be evidence-based, innovative, transparent and simple. We look forward to working with you.

Khanh Duy Russo

Director of Strategic Partnerships and Performance

Khanh Duy Russo is a Sr. Policy Advisor and the Mayor's LGBT Liaison to the Community. In that role, he develops a shared agenda and strategies with partners that leverages community resources and investments for creative, innovative public problem solving. Prior to joining the Mayor's office, he worked at Cisco where he guided the global strategy and implementation fo internet and technology solutions addressing critcal human needs. This included strategies to alleviate hunger and homelessness, increase access to water and disaster relief. He holds a Master of Science in Public Policy and Management from Carnegie Mellon University, a Bachelor's in Political Science and Religious Studies from Santa Clara University, and was a Coro Northern California Public Affairs Fellow. Khanh speaks fluent Vietnamese and is conversant in Spanish. He and his husband currently reside in Downtown San José.

Nicholas Almeida

Chief Service Officer

Nick Almeida has more than 15 years of experience in both social and public finance, and a depth of experience providing strategic, financial and operational advisory services to private, public and social sector clients. Nick is the Co-Founder and Managing Director of Sidecar Social Finance (SSF)–a social impact agency providing impact investing advisory and capital services to individuals, institutions, and social entrepreneurs. He holds an MA in Sociology and a BA in Public Policy from Stanford University.

Ingrid Holguin

Chief Learning Officer

Ingrid Holguin joined the Office of the Mayor of San José, Sam Liccardo as Policy Advisor for Strategic Partnerships in early 2015. Ms. Holguin has over 20 years of experience working on issues of economic and community development with a passion for building financial capabilities for individuals and institutions. Most recently she was Director of Savings Programs for Opportunity Fund, a San José-headquartered Community Development Financial Institution. As a consultant for over ten years and prior work as Program Officer for the Ford Foundation in Mexico City, working in Mexico and Central America, her work has included philanthropy, impact investing, strategic planning, evaluation and program design. Ms. Holguin holds an MBA from the University of California – Berkeley, Walter A. Haas School of Business and a B.A. in Economics and Management from Ball State University. She speaks fluent Spanish, lives in San José with her husband and teenage daughter and is proud mother of an AmeriCorps VISTA Teaching Fellow at an East San José middle school extended learning program.

Anna Le

Transportation Specialist

Anna serves as a Fund Development and Strategic Planning Manager for the Vietnamese American Community Center.

Anna brings over 20 years of experience in project management, community outreach, economic and business development, fund development, strategic planning, organizational leadership, community engagement, administration, human resources, budgeting, purchasing, grant writing, and event management. Her diverse experience includes demonstrated excellence in establishing and maintaining working relationships with private and public sectors, nonprofits, elected officials, coalitions, consultants, developers, real estate and financing professionals, contractors, volunteers and other stakeholders and organizations. Her experience includes being Administrative /Human Resource Manager with Everett Charles Technology, Associate Developer with Eden Housing, Development Officer with San José Redevelopment Agency, and Program Manager with San José Department of Transportation.

She has a MS degree in Urban and Regional Planning and a BS in Mathematics, both from San José State University, and speaks fluent Vietnamese. She resides in San José with her husband and two children.

Dima Khoury

Mayoral Fellow

An accomplished business operations leader with extensive background in technology. Dima has over 20 years of experience in high tech companies, leading software engineering teams as Director of engineering and as a Director of business operations. Dima retired from Cisco October 2014 and is now focused on leveraging her knowledge and experience for the nonprofit sector. In her role as an Encore Fellow in the office of Mayor Liccardo, Dima is the Campaign director for San José Generation to Generation campaign, developing strategies for inter-generational programs in the community. Dima is also a mentor at the Miller center at Santa Clara University coaching social entrepreneurs through the Global Social Benefit Institute, which is the accelerator program of the Miller center. Dima earned her bachelor degree from Universite Paul Sabatier, Toulouse, France in 1988 and her Master’s in engineering from Santa Clara University in 1992.

Candace Le

Policy Advisor for Education and Vietnamese Relations

Candace joined Mayor Liccardo’s team in 2016 as the liaison to the Vietnamese community and recently added education initiatives to her portfolio. A native Vietnamese speaker, Candace is deeply rooted in and connected to the Vietnamese-American community locally. At UC Berkeley, Candace studied sociology with an emphasis on the impact of education in minority communities. Prior to joining Mayor Liccardo’s team, Candace was the program coordinator for an afterschool youth development program at Asian Americans for Community Involvement (AACI). Candace continues to be involved as an organizer with community-based organizations and works to connect her direct-service understanding and local government experience to better serve the diverse San José community.
